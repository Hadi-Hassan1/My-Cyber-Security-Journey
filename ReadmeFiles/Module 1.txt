What I Learned:

* Learnt about Offensive Security which includes penetration testing, red teaming, and finding vulnerabilities before attackers do.

* Understood the 5 phases of penetration testing: 
Reconnaissance, Scanning, Exploitation, Post-Exploitation, and Reporting.

* Learnt about Defensive Security which focuses on protecting systems through:
SOC (Security Operations Center), Digital Forensics, Incident Response, and Malware Analysis.

* Understood the Incident Response phases: 
Preparation, Detection & Analysis, Containment, Eradication, Recovery, and Post-Incident Activity.

* Explored different career paths in cybersecurity including:
Penetration Tester, Red Team Operator, Security Analyst, Incident Responder, Threat Hunter, and many more with their salary ranges and required skills.

* Learnt about important certifications like Security+, CEH, OSCP, and their importance in career development.

Key Takeaways:

* Grasped the fundamental difference between offensive security (attacking to find weaknesses) and defensive security (protecting systems).

* Understood that ethical hacking requires written authorization and staying within defined scope.

* Realized there are many career options in cybersecurity with good salary potential ranging from $55k to $180k+.

* Learned that both technical skills (networking, Linux, programming) and soft skills (communication, report writing) are important.
